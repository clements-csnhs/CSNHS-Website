Community Projects
Listed below are the various projects CSNHS has completed, along with a brief description of what problem was solved, how it was solved, and what skills or technology was used to solve it. If you are a member interest in helping with one of the ongoing or soon to be starting projects, contact an officer to show your interest. If you are a teacher, club, or organization and have an idea for a project that you want help with, feel free to email us your idea at ping@clementscsnhs.org.
The RoundUp Website Refresh
The RoundUp is Clements High School's very own student newspaper publication. The RoundUp's members decided they wanted to revamp their website, so they came to CSNHS for help with designing and developing a new website for their newspaper. The two organizations are currently working together to create an updated, modern, and handy website for the RoundUp.

Counselor Technology Board
The Clement's High School Counselor's Office requested help from CSNHS to create a poster board to display various useful information, links, and forms to organize all the resources student's have at their disposal and help students know where to find them. CSNHS created a technology board for the counselor's that achieves all this, and more.

PE Fitnessgram Forms
Helped PE teacher collect information on student activities. Among the many changes caused by the pandemic and online learning was the shift in teaching of Physical Education classes. Clement's PE Coaches needed a way to efficiently and effectively gather information from their students on how much exercise they've done. CSNHS helped by creating Google Forms that allowed students to easily submit their weekly exercise and designing Spreadsheets that organized the information so the coaches could easily read and utilize the information.

March Madness Bracket
The March Madness Bracket Competition is a fun tradition that allow the Clements High School faculty to compete against eachother in a entertaining, friendly competition. However, the process of organizing and assigning points to the 60+ picks of each member of the faculty was a very strenuous, manual process. CSNHS solved this issue by designing spreadsheets that made organizing all the faculty's picks, assigning points based on game outcomes, and declaring winners quick and seamless.

Houstonfest Scripts
The Houstonfest German contest is a massive statewide contest that involves hundred's of schools and teams. Yet for years, the process of registering schools and teams was done manually. CSNHS helped out by creating an entire system for the contest that completely automated and handled the strenuous process of registering schools and teams.
